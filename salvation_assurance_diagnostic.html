Jesus said "by their fruit you will know them." This section examines the nine fruits of the Spirit as observable, measurable evidence of genuine transformation — the primary character dimension of the diagnostic.

Character — Primary
🔑 Tala ng Pasilitador / Facilitator Note

The fruit of the Spirit is not about perfection — it is about direction and pattern. You are looking for a consistent trajectory of growth, not a spotless record. Pay attention to which fruits the person rates highest and lowest. Low scores in multiple areas over a long period of church attendance may indicate either (a) a formation deficit, (b) an unresolved sin pattern, or rarely (c) an unconverted heart. The follow-up questions are the most important part of this section.

Rate each fruit of the Spirit as it currently describes your consistent daily life. Be honest — rate what is true now, not what you aspire to.

Ang mga Pagsubok sa 1 Juan — Anim na Marka ng Tunay na PananampalatayaThe 1 John Tests — Six Biblical Markers of Genuine Faith
1 John 1:6–7 · 2:3–4 · 2:15 · 3:6 · 3:14 · 4:7–8 · 5:1–4

The apostle John wrote his first letter explicitly so believers could know they have eternal life (5:13). He gives six repeating tests throughout the letter. This section applies all six.

Character — Primary
🔑 Tala ng Pasilitador / Facilitator Note

John's six tests are: (1) Walking in the light, (2) Keeping God's commands, (3) Not loving the world, (4) Practicing righteousness, (5) Loving other believers, (6) Believing in Jesus. Notice that these tests are about consistent pattern and direction, not perfection. A person who scores low on multiple tests consistently, especially tests 1 and 5, warrants a deeper pastoral conversation before placement in any ministry role.

Not true of me
Very true of me
