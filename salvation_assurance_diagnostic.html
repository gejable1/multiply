Purpose of this instrument: This diagnostic is not a test to pass or fail. It is a pastoral conversation tool — a mirror held up so that you and your discipler can see clearly what God has done, is doing, and is yet to do in your life. It is designed to uncover both character (who you are becoming in Christ) and competence (what you can do as a disciple). Answers are confidential. There are no trick questions. Honest responses are the only ones that help.

How to Use This Instrument

  1. Complete all sections honestly, without coaching from others beforehand.
  2. For rating questions: 1 = Not at all / Never  |  2 = Rarely  |  3 = Sometimes  |  4 = Often  |  5 = Consistently / Always
  3. For open-ended questions, write freely — there are no wrong answers.
  4. The facilitator section (gold border) is read and completed by the discipler, not the respondent.
  5. A debrief conversation with your discipler or pastor follows within 7 days of completion.

Assurance of Salvation — Do You Know That You Know?
1 John 5:11–13 · Rom 8:16 · John 10:28–29 · Heb 10:22

Assurance is not arrogance — it is the confident trust in God's promises, not one's own performance. This section distinguishes genuine assurance from presumption or uncertainty.

Character
🔑 Facilitator Note

Many Filipino believers live in chronic uncertainty about their salvation, often rooted in performance-based theology ("I must be good enough"). Others have presumption ("I was baptized so I'm fine"). Neither is healthy. You are looking for confident, grace-based assurance — faith resting on God's promise, not personal achievement. Low scores here don't necessarily mean no salvation; they may indicate a formation need around the doctrine of assurance.

Character — Primary
🔑 Facilitator Note

Pay attention to which fruits the person rates highest and lowest. The follow-up questions are the most important part of this section.

The 1 John Tests — Six Biblical Markers of Genuine Faith
1 John 1:6–7 · 2:3–4 · 2:15 · 3:6 · 3:14 · 4:7–8 · 5:1–4

The apostle John wrote his first letter explicitly so believers could know they have eternal life (5:13). He gives six repeating tests throughout the letter. This section applies all six.

Character — Primary
🔑 Facilitator Note

John's six tests are: (1) Walking in the light, (2) Keeping God's commands, (3) Not loving the world, (4) Practicing righteousness, (5) Loving other believers, (6) Believing in Jesus. Notice that these tests are about consistent pattern and direction, not perfection. A person who scores low on multiple tests consistently, especially tests 1 and 5, warrants a deeper pastoral conversation before placement in any ministry role.

Open Reflection — Your Own Voice
Ps 139:23–24 · Lam 3:40 · 2 Cor 13:5

These final questions invite honest self-reflection and personal expression. There are no right or wrong answers. The quality and honesty of these responses often reveal more than all the rating questions combined.

Character & Competence
Narrative Prompt H1 — Your Spiritual Autobiography

In the space below, write a brief spiritual autobiography — the story of your journey with God from the earliest you can remember to today. Include: the spiritual environment you grew up in, the key turning points, the seasons of closeness and distance from God, and where you are right now.

There is no word limit. Write as much or as little as you feel is honest and complete.
